A Cryptography Challenge

Used in the UNCC 2016 Cyber Security Symposium CTF


A text file named Psalm119 is given with a clue that reads:

xtra slim rev oft writes he kvetches old zoo brew


The file name is the first clue. A quick visit to the Wikipedia entry for Psalm 119 tells us that this psalm was also an acrostic in which taking the first letter of each stanza gives you the Hebrew alphabet.

The words in this challenge are simply encoded with an Atbash substitution cipher, originally used with the Hebrew alphabet. Decoding them gives us:

cgpz horn ive lug dirgvh sv pevgxsvh low all yivd


Remebering that the psalm was an acrostic and looking at the first letters of the above
message gives the answer: childsplay

Two additional clues are provided in the original text for anyone needing additional help.
The sixth and last words combined yield hebrew and kvetches is a Yiddish word.
